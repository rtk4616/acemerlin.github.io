That's it for my first job

Posted on 2017-07-05   |   In Life

I’ve worked for almost two years now. I just quit my first job not long ago, and today is also my birthday which makes me feel like to write something. Shit happened, things I learned. I’ve got a lot to tell and fellas I gotta say I’m quite disappointed with the whole industry.

My first job after graduation is Android Development which I must say it’s not a bad job. I worked with great people who help me go through the very early stage of fitting in, and people who taught me how to do development. I’d like to give special thanks to them. So it’s obvious that the reason I quit my first job is not I don’t prefer them, rather it’s the whole industry I dislike.

It’s common sense that’s only people shares the same interests/value would like each other. It’s the same with developers. Back in college, when I want to build something, like a blog site, I’m willing to spend time and dive into it to figure out how to do that:

  • what’s HTML/CSS/JavaScript and how to write them?
  • what’s Bootstrap, jQuery, CoffeeScript?
  • what’s an HTTP server, what’s Nginx?
  • what’s a Web Framework, like Play!/RoR/Django?
  • what’s the difference between those frameworks?
  • what’s MySQL, PostgreSQL, Redis, Memcached?
  • how to write Scala/Ruby/Python?
  • what’s Functional Programming?
  • what’s Lambda Calculus?
  • how to write Lisp?
  • how to use Emacs?
  • how to write Emacs Lisp?
  • how to solve the questions from SICP?
  • what’s DSL?
  • how to write Lisp macro?
  • how to write a Lisp interpreter?
  • what’s Tokenizer, Parser?
  • what’s typeclass?
  • how to write Haskell?
  • what’s Monad?
  • what’s Category Theory?
  • what’s Dependent Type?
  • how to write Coq?
  • how to solve the questions from Software Foundations?
  • …
  • how to own a domain?
  • how to use DigitalOcean or Heroku to hold my site?

When I need to learn front-end stuff, I just learn. When I need to learn Scala or any other language, I just learn. When I need to know new concepts like Functional Programming, I just learn. This list is still expanding. I’m that kind of people who like to learn, and what’s more, can get shit done. Only with enough breadth of knowledge can one have a clear vision of what’s going on inside Computer Science, and then grow his/her real interest, otherwise, one will surprise at anything new, and lost interests after know how it works(or just stay ignorance). I find my own interests particularly on PLT(Programming Language Theory). Sadly, after working for a while, I find out that people are just learning for the sake of work. Most of them refuse to learn anything new as long as their current knowledge satisfies their job. The only new knowledge they would learn is when something is gonna help him/her get a job in the future, and the majority of them plan to stick to the same field for the rest of their career!

What I’m saying is not that one should not stay focus only on his own field. What I’m saying is that they should not refuse the knowledge they don’t know, and what’s more, judge someone’s technical skills just by the stupid fucking title. They don’t care that I can efficiently write beautiful code using my functional programming skills(mostly because they never heard about it!!!), neither do they give a damn about my understanding of programming languages because other languages aren’t related to Android development. I’ve been using Kotlin as Android development language since 2015. Since that time, I tried my best to convince them that it’s a good language and have zero risks of using it. I mean, they sure surprised me with their stubbornness. Not until recently did they start actually looking into this language because Google announced it as the official Android development language. Wow, just wow, you refused to believe me because of what? I’m a kid who just graduated from school? One specific person laughed at me when I told him about how good Functional Programming is, not because he knew it very well and thought it’s bad, but rather he never heard of it and thinks it’s useless because he was already pretty good at Android development. That was the time I thought “I’m enough of your people’s shit. I’m outta here.”.

I’m not blaming them, really. I’m sure they are all good developers. It’s the industry’s fault, the industry makes hard working people into money making machine. They work so hard that there is no room and time for a litter dream. Well, the easiest way to deal with messy stuff is to not to deal with it. So, I quit.

Someone tells me that it’s the same everywhere, but we’ll see.
